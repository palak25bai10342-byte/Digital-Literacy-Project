 Cyberbullying Case Study 

Cyberbullying is a type of online harassment where a person is targeted through social media, messaging apps, or other digital platforms. It usually involves spreading rumors, sending abusive messages, or posting embarrassing content about someone.

A common example can be seen among students. For instance, a girl in a college group chat was targeted by a few classmates who started making fun of her posts and appearance. It began with sarcastic comments, but gradually turned into sharing edited images and creating memes about her. Someone even created a fake account in her name and posted inappropriate content, which made the situation worse.

This type of behavior often targets students and teenagers who are active online. People who are new in a group or are less confident are more likely to be targeted.

The consequences of cyberbullying can be very serious. The victim in this case started feeling stressed and avoided social media completely. Her confidence dropped, and it also affected her focus on studies. In some cases, such situations can even lead to anxiety and isolation.

This shows how important it is to behave responsibly online and think before posting anything that could hurt someone
